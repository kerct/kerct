Laser Tag++

Together with 3 other teammates, I developed an augmented reality laser tag game. Besides shooting their opponents, players can make hand gestures, such as a grenade throwing action, to cause damage to their opponents. I designed and built the hardware system, which consists of a gun, a vest, and a glove per player. The shooting mechanism is implemented using an IR emitter, integrated into the gun, together with an IR receiver, fitted onto the vest. An LED strip was also added onto the gun and the vest for visual effects. To detect the hand gestures, an inertial measurement unit on the glove collects data which will be filtered and processed before feeding into an AI classification network. The sensors are connected to Beetle BLEs, which are tiny Arduino boards, and communcation is done using Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) to a Linux device.

Wing Tester

In order to measure drag and lift forces on a wing easily, I developed an end-to-end system for the University of Toronto's Human Powered Vehicle Design Team. From drawing the schematic and assembling the circuit board to programming a graphical user interface, this system allows users to simply plug in the load cells and a laptop to read the values in real-time. Through the interface, users can also calibrate each load cell and export the data easily.

RTOS-Based Robotics Car

Together with 2 other teammates, I built a robotics car that can be remotely controlled through an Android app, via Bluetooth. Using the RTOS concept, we also enhanced the car with blinking LEDs, a buzzer, and an ultrasonic sensor. Check out our project video here!
